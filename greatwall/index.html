Brief view:

The Great Wall, also known as the Great Wall of China, is an ancient Chinese military fortification, a tall, strong, and continuous wall that was used to restrict the movement of enemy horsemen. The Great Wall is not just an isolated wall, but a defense system that combines a large number of walls, barriers, pavilions and markers. The history of the construction of the Great Wall dates back to the Western Zhou Dynasty, from which the famous story of "Beacon and Fire", which took place in the capital city of Haojing (present-day Xi'an, Shaanxi), originated. During the Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States Period, the Great Wall entered its first climax, but the length of the wall was relatively short. After Qin destroyed the six kingdoms and unified the country, Qin Shi Huang connected and repaired the Great Wall of the Warring States, which became known as the Great Wall of 10,000 miles. The Ming Dynasty was the last dynasty to repair the Great Wall, and most of the Great Wall that we see today was built at this time.

During the Qin Dynasty, there was a woman named Meng Jiangnu, soon after her husband was dragged to build the Great Wall, and there was no news of her for several years. Meng Jiangnu was so grief-stricken that she cried down the Great Wall. When Qin Shi Huang found out about this, he threatened her, but Meng Jiangnu pretended to agree to meet her husband for the last time, and after putting on her cold clothes, she crashed to death on a rock.
